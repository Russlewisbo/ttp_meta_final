üìä Project Overview

This project presents a comprehensive Bayesian meta-analysis examining the association between blood culture time-to-positivity (TTP) and clinical outcomes in bacteremia patients. The analysis includes 42 effect sizes from 40 unique studies with structured data extraction and rigorous statistical methods.

üéØ Key Findings

Primary Result: Shorter TTP is strongly associated with increased mortality (OR = 2.14, 95% CrI: 1.69‚Äì2.79, P(OR > 1) = 100%)
Critical Discovery: TTP is a pathogen-agnostic prognostic marker ‚Äî virtually identical associations for Gram-positive (OR = 2.17) and Gram-negative (OR = 2.06) infections. TTP can inform decisions before organism identification.
Robustness: The effect is consistent across all Bayesian sensitivity analyses, prior specifications, and publication bias adjustments. Persistent bacteremia shows an even stronger signal (OR = 4.02).

üíä Clinical Implications

For Clinicians: Short TTP identifies high-risk bacteremia patients. Use TTP for risk stratification regardless of gram stain result. Patients with TTP <10-12h are at ~2√ó higher odds of mortality and warrant closer monitoring.
For Researchers: Standardize TTP cutpoints in future studies (recommend 10-12h). Report both continuous and dichotomized data. More studies needed on clinical failure outcomes with consistent definitions.
Key Insight: The choice of TTP cutpoint significantly affects observed effect magnitude. Very rapid bacterial growth (short TTP, <10-12h) has the strongest prognostic value. Higher cutpoints dilute this signal.
